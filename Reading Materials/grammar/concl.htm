Conclusion

One reason we looked at language was to obtain a general view of the interesting and scientific way in which rules relating to language are described in ancient Indian texts. We must appreciate that language initially evolved through spoken language and then these grammarians tried to describe an already existing language on the basis of scientifically designed rules. Panini, in his work, concentrated on brevity and hence used many devices and references to achieve his aim. Tholkappiyar on the other hand,   was more interested in giving detailed description and hence,  many of his rules are very long.

An interesting offshoot of studying language theory has been the thought of adopting these scientific rules to make computers understand language. The scientifically designed rules are easily adaptable to computing and hence can form a basis for understanding and generation of these languages.
