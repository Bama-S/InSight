Panini's Ashtadhyaayi

As the name indicates this work consists of eight adhyaayas (chapters).  It is  also called  shabdaanushaasana.  They are organised in the form of Sutras.  What are Sutras? Sutras are concise statements. They have the property of being short, unambiguous, comprehensive, relevant and blemishless. Most Indian works are expressed as sutras in a compact manner. Panini's Ashtaadhyaayi is one classic example of this style. Just imagine, Panini has defined the whole of Sanskrit grammar using only 4000 sutras !

The subject matter discussed in Panini's work is not arranged topic-wise but in a manner such that it covers the entire grammatical rules unambiguously in 4000 sutras.  For achieving this, Panini uses a number of technical mechanisms.  These can be understood through what are called Vidhis.
