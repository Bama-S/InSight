Word ordering in English

As we have already mentioned, grammar is a category of rules. Try to remember the English grammar that you studied in Wren and Martin. Basically, grammar is about the ordering of words in a sentence. In English, the word order is very critical and hence, this category of rules is very important.
Now let's look at an example in English.

The man ate the mango in the plate.

In this sentence 'The man' is the noun phrase, where 'man' is the noun and 'ate the mango in the plate' is the verb phrase where 'ate' is the verb.

Generally, a sentence consists of a noun-phrase and a verb phrase.The noun phrase in turn consists of a determiner, followed by zero or more adjectives, followed by a noun. The noun phrase can also be a noun phrase followed by a prepositional phrase, as in "The man with a coat", where "with a coat" is a prepositional phrase having "with" as a preposition followed by a noun-phrase.

The verb phrase can be a verb followed by a noun phrase or a prepositional phrase.

The rules we have defined above are only a sample. We also have special rules for passive and interrogative sentences.

IMPORTANCE OF THE ORDER OF PHRASES

   English is very strict about the order of the various phrases.  This means that if we change the order of the phrase the meaning of sentence changes. Following example illustrates this clearly.

      The big man ate the cake in the dish.

If the order is changed like this,

       The cake ate the big man in the dish.

you see how the meaning of the sentence changes.
