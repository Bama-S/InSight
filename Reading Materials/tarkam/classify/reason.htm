Logic as you must be knowing, or atleast will be knowing shortly, is as old as the oldest of sciences.  Take a second off,  and think how you came to know the world around you,  the way you know it today.  In the first place, most of us would say, 'Well, the people around us made us know it that way."  And that's pretty much the story.   But was it pure data that was put into your mind?  Of course, not!  Over the years not only did you learn to perceive things around you by classifying them based on their  hundred-million properties but you also developed the ability to reason about  their occurrence or their non-occurrence. This is precisely what logic is. Thus you see, logic is something that comes to us naturally over the years.

Nyaaya Shastra  is that Indian science which deals with logic and reasoning.  It has two schools of thought - the nyaayaa and the vaisheShikaa.   The founder of nyaayaa was sage Goutama while the sage KaNaada founded vaisheShikaa.  Together, these two are commonly referred to as the nyaaya-vaisheshika system.This may be also be referred to as Tarka Shastram

Nyaaya-shastra is not the work of a single person.  It has evolved over time, as the outcome of constant contemplation by many Indian logicians and thinkers.  It aims to probe into the ultimate truth, by exploiting our ability to reason things out.  It perfects this innate ability in us, with a certain set of rules and axioms.  With this, we can analyse and understand anything that we can think of. Moreover, the ingredient of this entire system is only common sense. This helps us to understand things pretty easily.

'tarka-sangraha' is an elementary work on nyaya-shastra that explains logic and reasoning in simple terms for the layman. It contains the elements of both Nyaya and VaisheShika schools of thought. This has been used as text for this course.

As this course unfolds, we shall take a two fold approach in studying tarkashastram.  The first is classification where  we group things based on the similarity of their characteristics.   It can be similarities in terms of action they perform, the qualities they possess, etc.   Then we look into the various ways by which the above characteristics can be used to arrive at a logical conclusion or otherwise referred to as inferencing.   Precisely what Sherlock Holmes uses to solve his cases !!
