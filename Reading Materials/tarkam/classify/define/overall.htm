Process of Reasoning and Understanding

Understanding the unknown was like stepping into a blackhole. The Indian thinkers who had already done this saved us from this trouble; they felt a comprehensive yet simple methodology was the need of the day and postulated the following stages in pursuit of the unknown.

  • Perception and verbal testimony (initial stage):

Perception is awareness of a thing or event through the senses; whatever one perceives is open to discussion. But Verbal testimony is that fact which is accepted without question. It comes from a reliable source, for example a teacher.
Axioms are typical examples of verbal testimony.

  • Investigation of   facts, correlation and discussion with the  help of reasons (second stage):

The observations of the earlier stage go through a series of discussions and analyses.  In these discussions one tries to reason out the cause and effect of such observations.  The correct observations with the incorrect reasons are possible candidates for outright rejection.   Only those observations that are supported by appropriate reasoning are accepted.

'The sun appears to go around the earth causing day and night.'
The above statement is perfectly valid when it is the question of the observation.  A person observing from earth obviously sees the sun going around the earth.
But this being a cause of day and nights is a false or an incorrect reason.

  • Contemplation leading to an inference   (third stage):

The accepted observations and reasons are studied and constantly contemplated by the human mind to arrive at a logical conclusion.  Once inferred this may be used in inferring other facts at a later date.

What we have done in classification is accomplishment of the second stage. Now that we have a method of understanding the things around us , we have to correlate those things that we perceive and those that we already know.  This is where cognition comes into picture.
