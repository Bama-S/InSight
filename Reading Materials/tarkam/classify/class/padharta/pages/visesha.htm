Particularity

The fifth category is particularity or 'visheSha'  (pronounced as vi-she-Sha).
It enables us to perceive things as different from one another.

All objects have a unique property of their own,which constitutes their particularity.
It is opposed to Saamaanya.

- Saamaanya is inclusive; where as visheSha is exclusive.
- Saamaanya forms the basis of universalisation; while visheSha forms the basis of discrimination .

The need   to distinguish objects arises when they are similar to each other, but not one and the same.

It is very important to know that objects are not distinguished by their particularities but by the difference between their particularities.

Thus for example,
one atom is similar to another atom of the same element. But, one atom differs from another not only in numerical existence but also in qualitative existence.

Each partless ultimate substance has an original peculiarity of its own called visheSha. In case objects can be further sub-divided then each such atomic subdivision will have its own particularity.

Though it is of a qualitative nature, and exists in a substance, yet it forms a distinct category.

Like atoms, space, time and manas all have their particularities.
