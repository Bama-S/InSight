कर्म

Action

The third category under classification of padhaartaas is action.
It is referred to as 'karma' in Sanskrit, pronounced as 'kar-maa'.
Like quality, action too needs a substance for its existence.  It is performed either by or on the substance.
While a quality is a static and permanent feature of a substance, an action is its dynamic and transient feature. Action is said to be of five kinds whose Sanskrit equivalents are:

Sanskrit Equivalent Pronounced as
To throw up utkShepanam उत्क्षेपणं ut-kShe-paNam
To throw down apakShepanam अपक्षेपणं apa-kShe-paNam
To collect aakunchanam आकुंचनं aa-kun-ca-nam
To spread prasaranam प्रसरणं pra-sa-ra-Nam
To move gamanam गमनं ga-ma-nam

To throw up - use of force to move an object or a substance up to a height.
Some example are - throwing a ball up, launching a rocket, jumping.

To throw down - use of force to move down an object or a substance from a height.
Some examples are - a lift moving down, things falling down due to force of gravity.

To collect - the act of gathering one or more substances.
Some examples are - gathering news,  collecting sea shells,stamps,etc.

To spread - to scatter,extend or expand a substance to cover a wider area.
Some examples are - blooming of flower , to spread ghee on chappathi.

To move - transition of a substance from one place to another.
Some examples are - a moving train, pulling a door, pushing a car.

 

Other examples of actions are:
sprinkle, spray, run, walk, flatten, blot, read, assemble,
summon, pitch, reject, hurl, toss, play...........
