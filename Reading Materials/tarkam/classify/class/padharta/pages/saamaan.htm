सामान्य

Generality

The fourth category is generality or 'saamaanya' (pronounced as saa-maan-ya).
It is the common characteristic of things which fall into a related group.

saamaanya is universal in nature.  It is not about substances, but about their common characteristics.  It is one, though the individuals in which it resides are many (i.e.  common to many).

Consider the following examples.
There is the class-essence of the universal of man, called 'man-ness'. Going by the definition of saamaanya, 'man-ness' has the characteristic of being common to all men. Thus 'man-ness'  can be called as 'saamaanya' as it exists in every man.

It is of two types.

  •     Majority    -    Those characteristics that are prevalent in most of the objects.
  •     Minority    -    Those characteristics that are common to a set of few objects alone.

For example,
Indians may form the majority of the population in India. But they are minority in other countries.

If saamaanya brings out the commonality in substances,
then how does one distinguish between them ?

By learning about the unique properties of the substances, which we shall now discuss.
