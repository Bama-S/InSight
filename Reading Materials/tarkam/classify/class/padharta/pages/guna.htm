Quality

Quality is referred to as guna in Sanskrit, and is pronounced as gu-Naa

Guna cannot exist independently and always needs to exist in a substance.
Moreover, all substances require at least one significant guna.
Though it cannot exist independently, still it can be conceived(prameya), known(jneya), and named(abhidheya) independently of a substance, where it inheres. It includes both material and mental qualities.

As explained earlier, qualities are similar to the adjectives which we use to 'qualify' the dravyas.

Consider for instance, the human senses of sound,vision,taste,smell and touch.  Well, these are some of the things by which we distinguish objects.

Given below is the list of 24 qualities recognized by 'Vaiseshika'.

 

Color rupa That which can be perceived by the eye.
Taste rasa That which can be perceived by the tongue.
Smell gandha That which can be perceived by the nose.
Touch sparsha That which can be perceived by the skin.
Sound shabda That which can be perceived by the ear.
Quantity parimaaNa That which causes usage of words like many, more, little.
Distinctness prthaktva That which causes the expression of distinctness of one object from another. It is present in all 9 dravyaas.
Conjunction samyoga That which arises due to the combination of two or more objects.
Disjunction vibhaga That which arises due to loss of conjunction.
Remoteness paratva Expressed by how far one lies from another.
Proximity aparatva Expressed by how close things are.
Heavy gurutva Property in objects causing it to fall down.
Fluidity dravatva It is the property that results in flow.
Stickiness sneha Adhesiveness property
Number sankhya That property which enables counting.
Intellect buddhi That which causes the mind to reason things out.
Happiness sukha Pleasant state of mind
Sadness duhkha When the mind is in a state of worry.
Desire ichchaa Urge to satisfy ones needs.
Hatred dvesha Strong dislike towards something or someone.
Effort prayatna Will to do
Good dharma That which is relatively righteous for a person.
Bad adharma That which is not good for a person.
Conditioning samskaara That which comes from good practice.

One may wonder what's the need for such a long list of 'qualities' ! But this list is a mutually exclusive and collectively exhaustive one, under which all the worldly qualities fall.
