Definition of Real World Entities

Now that we have completed all the seven padhaartaas, lets try to define objects using the characteristics that they possess.  To do this first we list out their characteristics under each padhaartaa, and then we try to fit in a definition for that object.

The example mentioned here demonstrate different ways to define objects using their characteristics.

Rain

Substance Water content

Quality Spherical (shape)drops, small (quantity) droplets, heaviness of droplets (heaviness)
Action Fall down (action), Condensation (conjunction of water vapour)
Vishesha Causes sudden fall in temperature (cooling - special characteristic of rain)

So rain can be defined as 'Small,spherical droplets of water, becoming heavy due to condensation of water vapour, falling down from the sky causing a fall in temperature."

Some might wonder whether we really need such a comprehensive definition.  To those an easier definition shall be:

Rain is the downpour of water that accumulated as clouds.

In the second definition, there is a bit of incompleteness.  One cannot understand the concept of rain, until he/she understands the concept of clouds.  So we go about defining the cloud.

  1. Cloud is the accumulation of water vapour that comes down as rain.                 or

  2. Cloud is the accumulation of water vapour that earlier evaporated due to the heat of the sun.

Now we have come up with 2 definitions.  The first one defines clouds again in terms of rain.   But earlier we defined rain in terms of clouds.  This leads to some sort of a circling definition.  This defect is referred to as 'circling effect '.  A good definition should never suffer from such an effect.

In the second definition to understand the meaning of cloud, one needs to understand what evaporation and sun are.

So we move into the third level of definition.

  1. Evaporation is the process by which water, due to the heat of the sun, becomes vapour (i.e. water in gaseous state).

  2. Sun is a star and is a   source of heat and light.

Now the definition is complete, so we needn't go further.

To summarise,

  1. A good definition should never suffer from circling effect.

  2. It should be defined in a finite number of stages.
