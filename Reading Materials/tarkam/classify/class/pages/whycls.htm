Why Classification ?

.... Man in the pursuit of unraveling the mysteries of nature, has tried to understand the basic building blocks of the universe. Starting from the sun, the moon and the stars his enquiry has lead him to the atom , the nucleus ,electrons ,quarks and what not!

In this process, it is a whole gamut of information that he has collected, classified and analysed methodically - that we know the world as it is today.
The key words here are methodical and classification

What do we mean by 'methodical classification'?  
Here are a few examples which may help you to understand.

Consider the doctor next door .

He's specialised from a school of medicine that didn't have a sound classification for the various kinds of diseases . Nonetheless, our chap was a bright fellow and he made good guesses of what diseases his patients had . And , he had a reasonable amount of success ! So , will any of you people consult this genius ? The answer 'd be a firm NO if only you valued your life !

Imagine our plight if we didn't know classification in science ?

The advancements in biology, chemistry or for that matter, in any field ,is mostly due to the underlying sound system of classification .

For example, we classify

substances into solids , liquids and gases .
members of a species as either male or female,
lion, tiger, cheetah, etc as belonging to the cat family, and
wolf, fox, hyena, etc as belonging to the dog family.

This is a simple classification we do based on similarities of their build, behavior and appearance .

Another example is the use of classification in the preparation of maps that can be used to pictorially represent the characteristics of a place.

Now you can easily tell the properties of the element , like the number of electrons in their valence shells.
This helps predict the chemical properties of the elements such as the number of bonds they may have and whether they will behave as acids or bases. Similarly, compounds are classified as Organic and Inorganic depending on the presence of CH and CC bonds.
These two classifications given above show that classifications can be done individually or as a group.

Such is the power of classification. It helps to simplify the work of predicting the behavior of the items depending on their property.

To summarise, these are the advantages of classification:
We get a  better understanding of things around us.
We can relate things which have certain level of commonality.
Using classification makes inferencing easy.
We can comprehensively define things based on their features.
