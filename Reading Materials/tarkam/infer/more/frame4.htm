Exercises:

I. Try putting the following statements as a five-membered reasoning.

Identify the Pratjnaa, Hetu and find examples for the vyaapti (Udaaharana). Then, derive  the conclusion (nigamana) by applying the vyaapti to the present case (Upanaya).

1. "Light travels faster than sound because lightning reaches earth before thunder"

2. "Na reacts with Cl and loses an electron to form NaCl while Cl gains an electron in the reaction."

3. "Identification of the class to which the animal specimen belongs (say, how would you identify a specimen of fish?)."

4. "How is it that a ball thrown up, comes down?"

5. "How will you know, when the gas in the cylinder in your  house is about to get over?"

6. "The usual salt analysis stuff! How will you find if a given salt is a carbonate or not?"

Answers for some of the above need not be unique. One may infer a result by observing various phenomena. Just to see how we've inferred, click for Answers.

II.  Here are some problems/puzzles which you'll come across often. Solve them and explain the process of inference and the five members of paraartha anumaanam.

1.  If five points are placed in a square of side 2 units, then prove that atleast two would be separated by not more than 1 unit.

2. What comes next in the series M, A, M, J, J, A, S?

3.  A number whose last digit is '7 ' has a peculiar property. When its last digit (units place) is brought to the 1st place (Most significant place), we find that the new number obtained is five times the old number. Find the number.

III. Fit the theorems / proofs you study in school into the five-membered paraartha anumanam form.

IV. Find some more examples for various types of vyaapti.
