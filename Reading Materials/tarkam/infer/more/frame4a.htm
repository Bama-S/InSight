The answers given below consist of the three members pratijnaa, hetu and udaaharana. The upanaya and nigamana can be easily found out from the given three members.

1.

Pratijnaa : Light travels faster than sound.

Hetu : When it rains you see the lightning first and only then do you hear the thunder though both of them 'start' or rather originate at the same place .

Udhaharana : If two things start at some point and proceed towards you, and one reaches before the other, then you conclude that the former travels faster than the latter.

eg: as in a car race.

2.

Pratijnaa : Na reacts with Cl and loses an electron to form NaCl while Cl gains an electron in the reaction.

Hetu : This happens because Na is more electropositive when compared to Cl.

Udhaharana : All alkalis are electropositive and all halogens are electronegative and all alkali-halogen reactions result in the formation of alkali halides.

eg: as in the reaction Li + F ---> LiF.

3.

Let's say you've been given some animal specimen(say, a fish). You are asked to identify which class it belongs to. The reasoning can be done as follows.

Pratijnaa : The given specimen belongs to class Pisces.

Hetu : This's because the specimen's got a two-chambered heart, breathes with its gills and is an aquatic animal.

Udhaharana : All animals falling under this class have the above characteristics.

eg: as in some known fish  like salmon.

4.

Pratijnaa : A ball thrown up will come down.( It is thrown up with a velocity less than the escape velocity)

Hetu : That's because the ball is acted upon by the earth's gravity which causes it to come down.

Udhaharana : All objects thrown up are acted upon by the earth's gravity and hence come down.

eg: as when a stone is thrown up.

5.

Pratijnaa : The gas supply in your house is about to get over.(The stove is assumed to be perfect working condition.)

Hetu : You infer this because the flames aren't uniform and they break off repeatedly.

Udhaharana : Whenever in a system, if the output is irregular, then it means that there is something wrong with the input.

eg: i. A car running out of fuel,

     ii. Exam results of a student who wrote that exam without sufficient preparation!

6.

Pratijnaa : Given salt is a carbonate (pratijnaa)

Hetu : Produces effervescence in dil.HCl (hetu)

Udaaharana : If a salt produces effervescence in dil.HCl, it is a carbonate salt.

eg: calcium carbonate.
