GEOMETRY

Geometry is the domain of lines, circles, and other such objects used for construction. Geometry was highly developed in ancient India, as geometrical patterns were put to practical use in the construction of temples and other structures.

The meticulous prescriptions for these constructions   reveals a precise knowledge of several theorems of Geometry. We propose an intoduction to some of these in the discussions to follow.It includes theorems dealing with the properties of triangles and cyclic quadrilaterals.

There are several sutras or rules used in geometry, mainly those used in construction. These sutras were called 'sulbasutra'. The root word 'sulb' or 'sulv' means to measure and 'sulba' means measuring rope or cord.The katyayana Sulbasutras belong to the Sukla Yajurveda, and the remaining Sulbasutras to Krsna Yajurveda.The oldest Sulbasutras, the Bodhayana is said to be composed in 8th century B.C.
