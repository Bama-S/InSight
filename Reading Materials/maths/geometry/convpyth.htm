Converse of the Pythagorus theorem

Statement of the theorem :
If in a triangle ABC the sides BC=a, CA=b, AB=c satisfy the relation 
c2 = a2 + b2 ---- (1),
then the angle ACB = 90.

Proof :

Construct a Right triangle A'B'M' such that M'B' = a and M'A' = b and angle M' = 90.
Let A'B' = c'
Then by Phythagoras theorem we have ,
c' 2 = a2 + b2
Therefore , from (1) we have c' 2 = c2 i.e. c'= c.
Since c'= c, triangle ABC and A'B'M' are congruent.
Consequently, angle C = angle M' = 90.
