Other theorems :

The product of the sides divided by twice the altitude is the radius of the circle that passes through the vertices of the triangle.

In the figure shown below, we have to prove that
bc/2p = R.

Brahmagupta's proof :

Let M denote the midpoint of arc AC and M' that of the complementary arc, ABM'PC.
Note here, that MM' will be a diameter of the circle.
So, we have arc AB+arc BM'= arc M'P+arc PC.
Since, M'P || BC ,we have arc BM' = arc PC.
So arc AB = arc M'P.
Therefore, Angle ACB = Angle M'MP.( equal angles subtend arcs of equal length )
Therefore, ACD & M'MP are similar and so we have
AC/M'M = AD/M'P
b/2R = p/c (since M'P=AB=c)
bc/2p=R.
Hence, proved.
