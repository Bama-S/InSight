Cyclic quadrilaterals

What are cyclic quadrilaterals ? They are quadrilaterals in which the opposite angles equal 180 degrees. They can be inscribed in a circle. In the figure shown,

Angle A + Angle C = 180
Angle B + Angle D = 180

And, as in any other quadrilateral,
A + B + C + D = 360

Brahmagupta obtained a formula for the area of the cyclic quadrilateral in terms of its sides. He also found expressions for its diagonals. Brahmagupta's theorem about the diagonals of a cyclic quadrilateral was rediscovered by W.Snell in 1619 A.D.

The area of a cyclic quadrilateral is given in terms of its sides by:
area = sqrt((Q - a1)(Q - a2)(Q - a3)(Q - a4))
where 2Q = a1 + a2+ a3 + a4 and a1,a2,a3,a4 are the sides of the cyclic quadrilateral.

The formula for area of the triangle in terms of its sides was given by Heron of Alexandria. Brahmagupta's work on cyclic quadrilaterals is a special case of Heron's theorem. His mode of derivation of the theorem is entirely different from that of Heron.
