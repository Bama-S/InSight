Construction of right triangles

Katyayana gives the following construction for a right triangle :



If we construct an isosceles triangle with base BC = (n-1)a, and sides AB = AC = a. (n+1)/2 , then the altitude AP = sqrt(n).a  i.e., the square with side AP will yield n.a2.
(The value of n, being any integer starting from 1).
The value of AP can also be computed by the Pythagoras theorem.

Pythagorean Triplets

These are positive integers which form the sides of a right angled triangle. Some of these triplets were listed by Indian mathematicians Apasthamba and Bodhayana

Apasthamba :    (15,36,39), (12,16 20), (15,20,25), (5,12,13), (8,15,17), (12,35,37)

Bodhayana :       (3,4,5), (5,12,13), (8,15,17), (7,24,25), (12,35,37), (15,36,39)

Letting n=m2 in the triplet (a.(n-1) , a.sqrt(n), a.(n+1)/2) we get, the rational triplet
(a.(m2 -1), ma, a.(m2+1)/2).
