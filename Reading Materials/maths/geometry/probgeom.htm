Exercises in geometry :

  • In a triangle ABC if AB=sqrt(10) - sqrt(5), AC=sqrt(6) and BC=sqrt(18)-1,
    find the altitude from A onto BC.

  • Prove that area of  a triangle ABC = abc/4R, where R is the radius of the circle, which passes through the vertices of the triangle and, a,b,c are the sides of the triangle.

  • Derive the formula
    (a/sin A) = (b/sin B) = (c/sin C) = 2R
    for a triangle ABC, where a,b,c are the sides and R is the radius of the circle passing through the vertices (circumcircle).

  • Express a1a3+a2a4 in terms of the diagonals of a cyclic quadrilateral, where a1, a2, a3, a4 are the sides of the cyclic quadrilateral.
