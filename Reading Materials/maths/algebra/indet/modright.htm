Steps for formation of the table in Bhaskara's method :

In Bhaskara's method, it is not necessary to find a multiplier and a quotient as was done in Aryabhatta's method. The constant in the equation and 0 are added to the table, instead.

The number of rows of the table is the number quotients (number of rows of Column Q in Table 1), plus 2.

1) Column 1 of Rows 6,5,4,3 will contain the column Q of the above table.
2) Column 1 of Row 2 will contain the constant (=10) and that of Row 1 will contain 0.
3) Now, start from the bottom. (R1,C2) = 0,(R2, C2) = 10
4) (R3,C2) = (R2,C2) * (R3,C1) + (R1,C2)
    (R3,C2) = 10 * 1 + 0 = 10.
5) (R4,C2) = (R3,C2) * (R4,C1) + (R2,C2)
    (R4,C2) = 10 * 1 + 10 = 20
6) (R5,C2) = (R4,C2) * (R5,C1) + (R3,C2)
    (R5,C2) = 20 * 3 + 10 = 70
7) (R6,C2) = (R5,C2) * (R6,C1) + (R4,C2)
    (R6,C2) = 70 * 2 + 20 = 160

Therefore, x = 70 and y = 160.

In general, the contents of (Rn,C2) i.e. Row n,Column 2 (except the last 2 rows) will be (Rn-1,C2) * (Rn, C1) + (Rn-2, C2). 
Notice here, that we get a solution which is different from that obtained in Aryabhatta's method.
