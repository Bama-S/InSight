Solutions to exercises in indeterminate equations :

  • 120x + 49 = 31y
    120 > 31

    Hence, doing the repeated division, we get
    120 = 31 × 3 + 27
    31 = 27  × 1 + 4
    27 = 4  × 6 + 3
      4 = 3  × 1 + 1

    The number of quotients, excepting the 1st row is odd. Hence, to find the multiplier z,we use the equation
    z  × 1 - 49 = 3  × m
    z = 52 and m =1

    Forming the table, we get,
    3 423 ×3 + 370 = 1639
    1 370  × 1 + 53 = 423
    6 53  × 6 + 52 = 370
    1 52  × 1 + 1 = 53
    52 52
    1  

            Thus, we get the solution as x = 423 and y = 1639.

  • There are two numbers. The first number, when divided by 6, leaves a remainder of 4. The second, when divided by 11 leaves a remainder 7. The difference between the first and second numbers is 10. Find the numbers.

    Let the first number be x and the second number be y.
    x - y = 10.

    x = 6a + 4
    y = 11b + 7

    thus, x -y = 6a + 4 - (11b + 7) = 10
    i.e. 6a - 11 b - 3 = 10 (or) 11b + 13 = 6a.

    Forming the quotient table is common to both Aryabhatta's & Bhaskara's methods.
    11 > 6 .
    11 = 6 × 1 + 5
      6 = 5 × 1 + 1

    Aryabhatta's method:
    The number of quotients is 1, which can be considered odd. Now, to find a multiplier.
    1 × z - 13 = 5 × m
    z = 18 and m = 1
    1 19 × 1 + 18 = 37
    1 18 × 1 + 1 = 19
    18 18
    1  

    Thus, a = 37 and b = 19.

    x = 6 × 37 + 4 =226
    y = 11 × 19 + 7 = 216

    Bhaskara's method :
    Since there is no need to find a multiplier, we just go ahead with forming the table.

    1 13 × 1 + 13 = 26
    1 13 × 1 + 0 = 13
    13 13
    0  

    Thus, a = 26 and b = 13.

    x = 6 × 26 + 4 = 160
    y = 11 × 13 + 7 = 150

  • For 16, 3a, and 17b, find the values of a & b for which the three numbers are in A.P. Use Bhaskara's method
    Since 16, 3a and 17b are in A.P,
    2 × 3a = 16 + 17 b
    6a = 17b +16

    Forming the quotient table, we get,
    17 = 6 × 2 + 5
    6 = 5 × 1 + 1
    2 16 × 2 +16 = 48
    1 16
    16 16
    0  

    Thus, we get a = 48 and b = 16. The three numbers in A.P are 16, 144 and 272.

  • There are  four bases 2,5,7 and 9. Represent the number 303, as a quartet, in terms of these four numbers.
    [303] 2 = 1, which is the remainder when 303 is divided by 2.
    [303] 5 = 3, which is the remainder when 303 is divided by 5.
    [303] 7 = 2, which is the remainder when 303 is divided by 7.
    [303] 9 = 6, which is the remainder when 303 is divided by 9.

    Thus, 303 = (1, 3, 2, 6).
