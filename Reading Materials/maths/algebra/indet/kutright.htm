The number of rows in the table is the number of quotients (number of rows of Column Q of Table 1), plus 2.

1) Column 1 of Rows 6,5,4,3 will contain the column Q of the above table.
2) Column 1 of Row 2 will contain the multiplier (=18) and that of Row 1 will contain the quotient (=1).
3) Now, start from the bottom. (R1,C2) = 1,(R2, C2) = 18
4) (R3,C2) = (R2,C2) * (R3,C1) + (R1,C2)
    (R3,C2) = 18 * 1 + 1 = 19.
5) (R4,C2) = (R3,C2) * (R4,C1) + (R2,C2)
    (R4,C2) = 19 * 1 + 18 = 37
6) (R5,C2) = (R4,C2) * (R5,C1) + (R3,C2)
    (R5,C2) = 37 * 3 + 37 = 130
7) (R6,C2) = (R5,C2) * (R6,C1) + (R4,C2)
    (R6,C2) = 130 * 2 + 37 = 297

Therefore, x = 130 and y = 297.

In general, the contents of (Rn,C2) i.e. Row n,Column 2 (except the last 2 rows) will be (Rn-1,C2) * (Rn, C1) + (Rn-2, C2).
