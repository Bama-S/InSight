What is an indeterminate equation ?


  		Suppose you have Rs.100/- and you want to buy tomatoes and carrots. The cost of one tomato is Rs.8/- and a carrot costs Rs.5/-.How many tomatoes and carrots can you buy ?

 
Let us try to solve this mathematically:
Let 'x' be the number of tomatoes and 'y' be the number of carrots we can buy with Rs.100/-.
Therefore , we have,

    8x + 5y = 100

This equations looks familiar, doesn't it ? Well, it can be directly derived from the Chinese Remainder theorem.

We have one equation and two unknown quantities. If the number of unknown quantities is greater than the number of equations , there will be an unlimited number of solutions : these equations are called Indeterminate Equations.

Where was the need to solve indeterminate equations ?

Indian mathematicians came across these equations while solving problems in astronomy. The following was one of the problems that cropped up : -
The mean (position) of the sun (for sunrise) on a Wednesday is stated to be 8 signs, 25 degrees, 36 minutes and 10 seconds. Say exactly after how much time (since the beginning of Kaliyuga) will the Sun again assume the same position (at sunrise) on a Thursday, a Friday, and a Wednesday.
A method  called 'kuttaka', was devised  by mathematicians like Aryabhatta and Bhaskara, to solve such indeterminate equations.
