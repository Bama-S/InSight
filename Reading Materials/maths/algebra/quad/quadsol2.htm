Solutions to exercises in quadratic equations

  • 2x2 - 12x  + 16 = 0
    2 * 2 * x - 12 = ± sqrt (122 - 4 * 2 * 16)
    4 x - 12 = ± sqrt (144 - 128) = ± sqrt (16)
    4 x - 12 = ± 4
    4 x = 16 (or) 4 x = 8
    x = 4 or x = 2.

  • The sum of the ages of 2 sisters Sunita and Sujata,  is 18 and the product of their ages is 81. Prove that they are twins.
    The general form of a quadratic equation is
    x2 - (sum of roots) x  + (product of roots) = 0
    Let x represent the ages of the Sunita & Sujata. Since the sum of their ages is 18 & the product of the ages is 81, we have
    x2 - 18x  + 81 = 0
    2x - 18 = ± sqrt(182 - 4 * 1 * 81)
    2x - 18 = ± sqrt(324 - 324) = 0
    Hence both values of x = 9, which means both Sunita & Sujata are of the same age. This is possible only if they were born on the same day.So, they must be twins !

  • The problem stated in the verse is as follows :- There are several swans in a lake. 7/2 times the square  root of the total, is on the shore, while the remaining two are involved in a playful fight in the lake. What is the total number of swans ?

    Let the number of swans be n.
    (7/2) sqrt(n) + 2 = n

    Let sqrt(n) = y.
    (7/2)y + 2 = y2

    y2 - (7/2)y - 2 = 0

    2y2 - 7y - 4 = 0

    4y - 7 = ± sqrt (49 + 4 * 2 * 4)
    4y - 7 = ± sqrt (49 + 32) = ± sqrt (81)
    4y - 7 = ± 9
    4y = 16 or 4y = 2
    y = 4 or y = (1/2).

    y has to be an integer. Hence, y = 4.
    The number of swans is y2 = 42 =  16.
  • There are two numbers, x and y. The sum of their squares is 2.9 times their product. Find the ratio of the two numbers.

    x2 + y2 = (2.9) xy
    x2 + y2 = (29/10) xy

    Dividing the equation by xy, we get,

    (x /y ) + (y / x) = 29 /10

    Let (x/y ) = p. We get,

    p + (1/p) = 29 / 10

    Since the LHS is a sum of reciprocals, we write the RHS also as a sum of reciprocals.

    p + (1/p) = (5/2) + (2/5)

    p = 5/2  or 2/5, which is the ratio between the numbers.
