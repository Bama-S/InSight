Solution to quadratic equations

Now, let's get down to brasstacks and see how quadratic equations are solved using the Vedic approach.

Consider the equation
    x2 - 5x + 6 = 0

Take the expression 2x - 5

Equate it to the value of the discriminant which is ±  sqrt (5 2 - 4 * 1 * 6 ) = ± 1

2x - 5 = 1 and 2x - 5 = -1 ; hence x = 3 or x = 2

Explanation of the Vedic method :

  • For the equation ax2 + bx + c = 0, find  2 a x + b, which in this case is 2x -5.
  • Find the discriminant which is the square root of b2 - 4ac.
    Discriminant in this case is ±  sqrt (5 2 - 4 * 1 * 6 ) = ± 1
  • 2 a x + b = Discriminant
    In this case, 2x - 5 = +1 ; 2x - 5 = -1 ; hence, x = 3 or x= 2

The expression 2 a x + b is called the differential of the expression ax2 + bx + c. You will be coming across this term when you begin to learn calculus.

More Examples

  • x2 + 6x + 8 = 0
    2*1*x + 6 = ± sqrt (62 - 4*1*8)
    2x + 6 = ± sqrt (36 - 32) = ± sqrt(4) = ± 2
    Hence, 2x + 6 = + 2 (or) 2x + 6 = -2
    2x = - 4 (or) 2x = -8
    x = - 2 or x = - 4

  • 3x2 - 8x - 3 = 0
    2*3*x - 8 = ± sqrt ((-8)2 - 4*3*(-3))
    6x - 8 = ± sqrt (64 + 36) = ± sqrt (100)
    6x - 8 = ± 10
    6x - 8 = + 10 (or) 6x - 8 = - 10
    6x = 18 (or) 6x = -2
    x = 3 (or) x = - 1/3
