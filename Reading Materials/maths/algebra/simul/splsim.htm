What is so special ?

Well, there's something in these equations that makes them extremely simple to solve, saving you a lot of cerebral strain !

CASE 1 :

General rule : If the ratio of  coefficients of any one of  the unknown quantities is proportional to that of the constant quantities, then the value of the other unknown is 0.

Example 1: Consider the equations

    6x + 15y = 30  
    7x + 5y = 10                    

In the above two equations, the ratio of the coefficients of y are in proportion to that of the right hand side constants i.e. 15 / 5 = 30 /10. Hence, we can conclude that x = 0.

Substituting in either equation, we get y = 2.

Example 2: Consider the equations

       3x + 20y = 39
       6x + 23y = 78

The ratio of the x - coeeficients are the same as the ratio of the constants i.e. (3 /6) = (39/78). Hence, y = 0.

Substituting, we get x = 13.

CASE 2 :

General rule : When the x-coefficients and the y-coefficients are interchanged, performing just additions and subtractions on the set of equations will reduce them to a very simple form.

Example 1 : Consider the equations

15x + 23y = 53           --- eqn A
23x + 15y = 61           --- eqn B

Eqn A + Eqn B yields 38x + 38y = 114  which can be written as x + y = 3  (eqn C)
Eqn B - Eqn A yields 8x - 8y = 8 which can be written as x - y = 1 (eqn D)

Solving Eqns C & D, we get x = 2 and y = 1. 

Example 2 : Consider the equations

31x + 2y = 221            --- eqn A
2x + 31y = 76            --- eqn B

Eqn A + Eqn B gives 33x + 33y = 297 which is x + y = 9 (eqn C)
Eqn A - Eqn B gives 29x - 29y = 145 which is x - y = 5 (eqn D)

Solving equations C & D, we get x = 7 and y = 2.
