Inverse trigonometric functions

We know that sin (30) = 1/2.
The inverse of this sine function is denoted by sin-1 (1/2) = 30 degrees. The inverse function gives the angle whose trigonometric ratio is the number. Similarly, the other inverse functions can be defined.

Examples :

(1) To evaluate  sin-1(3/5) +  tan-1(2)

This is done very simply, by using the concept of Tribhujank.
Let sin-1(3/5) = A & tan-1(2) = B. So, we have to find A+B

The tribhujank for  A is  4,3,5
The tribhujank for  B is 1, 2, sqrt(5)

A 4 3 5
B 1 2 sqrt(5)
A+B -2 11 5 sqrt(5)

The -2 indicates that the angle is obtuse.

Thus A + B = tan-1 (-11/2).

(2) To find cot-1(2) +  cot-1(3)

Let cot-1(2) = A & cot-1(3) = B. So, we have to find A+B

The tribhujank for  A is  2, 1, sqrt(5)
The tribhujank for  B is 3, 1, sqrt(10)

A 2 1 sqrt(5)
B 3 1 sqrt(10)
A+B 5
1		 5

sqrt(2)

Thus A+B = 45 degrees.
