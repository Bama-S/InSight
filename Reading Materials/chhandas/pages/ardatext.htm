Now that we have looked at how to classify verses  belonging to Samavrttam, we shall move on to study about Ardhavrttam. 'Ardha' in Sanskrit means half. As we saw earlier, an Ardhavrttam has the I and III quarters in one metre, and II and IV quarters in another. This type of verse is not as widely used as Samavrttam.

    The main difference between samavrtaam on one hand and ardhavrttam & viShamavrttam on the other is that unlike samavrttam, the other two don't have 'classes', because more than one metre is used to identify a single type of such vrttam. The rules regarding the classification of ardhavrttam is the same as that of samavrttam, except for the fact that there are two gaNaa schemes (one for the odd quarter and one for the even quarter), rather than one, used to identify each type. So, a gaNaa scheme doesn't uniquely identify a type of vrttam.

As we have no addition to the rules already mentioned, we straightaway take an example :

सहसा विदधीत न क्रियां अविवेक: परमापदां पदं ।
वृणुते हि विमृश्य कारिणं गुण लुब्धा: स्वयमेव संपद: ।।
    
                       sahasaa vidadheeta na kriyaam              ( I quarter)
                       avivekaH paramaapadaam padam            ( II quarter)
                       vrNute hi vimrShyakaariNam                  ( III quarter)
                       guNalubdhaaH svayameva sampadaH      ( IV quarter)

  

Take the first quarter, and split it as usual :

                          sa ha saa vi da dhee ta na kri yaam
                    L  L    G   L  L     G   L   L   L     G

This gives the scheme (L L G) (L L G) (L L L) G , corresponding to gaNaas : sa sa ja ga.

Let's do the same to the second quarter :

                         a  vi ve kaH pa ra maa pa daam pa dam
                         L  L  G    G   L   L    G   L     G    L    G

This yields the scheme (L L G) (G L L) (G L G) L G , corresponding to gaNaas : sa bha ra la ga.

So, we identify this verse with 2 sets of metres : sa sa ja ga (for odd quarter) and sa bha la ga (for even quarter). This scheme has been named as वियोगिनी (viyoginee). Check that the same scheme holds for the III and IV quarters also.

Note that the 2 metres are of unequal length. It is not necessary in ardhavrttam that the 2 metres should be of same length. In fact, it is this flexibility that gives variety to such verses, and when used properly, as many distinguished poets have done, can be very pleasing to the ears.

Take another verse :

अथ मदन वधूरुपप्लवान्तं व्यसनकृशा परिपालयां बभूव ।
शशिन इव दिवातनस्य लेखा किरणपरिक्षय धूसराप्रदोषं ।।
                        atha madana vadhoorupaplavaantam
                        vyasanakrshaa paripaalayaam babhoova
                        shashina iva divaatanasya lekhaa
                        kiraNaparikshaya dhoosaraapradoSham

  

The first quarter is :

                     a tha ma da na va dhoo ru pap la vaan tam
                          L   L   L   L   L   L    G    L    G   L   G     G

This gives the scheme : ( L L L) ( L L L) ( G L G) ( L G G) , and the gaNaas are na, na, ra, ya.

The second quarter is :

                          vya sa na kr shaa pa ri paa la yaam ba bhoo va
                            L1   L  L   L    G    L  L   G   L   G     L    G      G2

(Note : 1. The syllable 'vya' is short, though the half syllable 'v' is preceded by 'ya'. As we said earlier, the half-consonant rule applies only when it comes after  the syllable.

            2. This indicates that we consider the last syllable 'va' to be long. Remember what we said earlier? If a syllable is short at the end of a quarter, it can optionally be treated as long, if it helps to group the syllables properly. Here, we see that in the fourth quarter, the last syllable is long. So, to equalise, we have made 'va' in the second quarter long. This can be applied only to the last syllable of a quarter, and nowhere else.)

This gives the scheme : ( L L L) ( L G L) ( L G L) ( G L G) G, and the gaNaas are na, ja, ja, ra, ga.

So, we have found out the 2 metres as : na, na, ra, ya and na, ja, ja, ra, la. This scheme is given the name of पुष्पिताग्रा (puShpitaagraa).
