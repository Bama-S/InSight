This is the third variety of vrttam. Here, all the 4 quarters have different metres. Not many verses of this kind are found in Sanskrit literature. So, we shall look into just one of the metres employed in this type. The most common metre of this type is called udgataa :

अथ वासवस्य वचनेन ,चिर वदनस्त्रिलोचनं ।
क्लांतिरहितमभिराधयितुं विधिवत्तपं:सि विदधे धनंजय: ।।

atha vaasavasya vachanena                       (I quarter)
ruchira vadanastrilochanam                      (II quarter)
klaantirahitamabhiraadhayitum                 (III quarter)
vidhivattapaamsi vidadhe dhananjayaH     (IV quarter)

  

Let's split the syllables as usual :

First quarter :

a tha vaa sa vas ya va cha ne na
L   L    G   L   G  L    L   L   G   L                  (Scheme : sa, ja, sa, la)

Second quarter :

ru chi ra va da nas tri lo cha nam
 L   L   L   L  L    G   L   G   L    G                (Scheme : na, sa, ja, ga)

Third quarter :

klaan ti ra hi ta ma bhi raa dha yi tum
   G    L  L  L  L   L    L    G    L   L   G         ( Scheme : bha, na, ja, la, ga)

Fourth quarter :

vi dhi vat ta paam si vi da dhe dha nan ja yaH
L   L    G   L    G    L  L  L    G    L    G    L   G      (Scheme : sa, ja, sa, ja, ga)

So, this metre, viz. udgataa, is identified by the set of 4 schemes : sa, ja, sa, la (I quarter), na, sa, ja, ga (II quarter), bha, na, ja, la, ga (III quarter), sa, ja, sa, ja, ga (IV quarter).
