There is a group of metres that is classified under samavrttam, but only half-obeys its rule. That is, instead of having the same metre in all the 4 quarters, it has one metre in 2 of its quarters, and a different one in 2 others. This group is called upajaati.

             Upajaati differs from ardhavrttam in 2 ways. One, it is not necessary that the 1st and 3rd quarters have one metre, and 2nd and 4th have another. Here, the 1st and 2nd quarters may have one metre, 3rd and 4th another. Or, 1st quarter alone can be in one metre and the rest in another metre. All these verses are classified under the name upajaati. Two, unlike ardhavrttam, in which the 2 metres may belong to different classes, in upajaati, the 2 metres must belong to the same class. (Experts differ in their views regarding this condition, with some holding that upajaati may include metres from adjacent classes - like a combination of 11 and 12 syllabled quarters. We shall right now stick to the first view).

Let us again summarise the differences between ardhavrttam and upajaati. As we noted earlier, upajaati has both the metres in the same class, unlike ardhavrttam where no such restriction applies. Moreover, in ardhavrttam, it is necessary that the 1st and 3rd quarters have one metre, and the 2nd and 4th have another. In upajaati, the pairing of quarters may be done in the following ways, such that :

(i) 1st & 2nd quarters have one metre, and 3rd & 4th another;
(ii) 1st & 3rd quarters have one metre, 2nd & 4th have another;
(iii) 1st and 4th have one metre, 2nd & 3rd have another, and,
(iv) one quarter alone has one metre, with other three quarters having another.

Note that case (ii) is actually a special case of ardhavrttam, where the 2 different metres belong to the same class.
