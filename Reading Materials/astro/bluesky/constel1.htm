CONSTELLATIONS...JEWELS OF THE NIGHT SKY

The sky has innumerable stars on it. This makes the task of identifying a particular star very difficult. So man devised a way to make the task easy. He grouped the stars into constellations, just like Mendeleev grouped elements (halides, alkalines, alkaline earths, etc.) to get a Periodic Table. A constellation is a group of stars, which forms a pattern in the sky. From ancient times, people have associated the shape of animals, humans and other objects with a constellation. Identifying these patterns is much easier than identifying a single star, which is nothing but a point in the sky. There are totally 88 constellations.

Among the 88 constellations, a few are well known. They serve as astronomic references for an observer of the sky to find the positions of other planets and stars. As you can see from the above picture which shows the relative positions of the constellations in the sky, you should have identified that tese constellations resemble some familiar objects. For example, consider a few important ones:

  • The most important one is the Saptharishi Mandala, which resembles a 'Great Bear'.
  • The Bhootapa resembles the shape of a kite. 
  • The Vrishaba resembles a cow.
  • The Mesha resembles sheep.
  • The Simha resembles a lion.
  • The Kanya resembles a maiden.

There are many other constellations like these. But it will be difficult for a beginner to identify their shapes. A few are listed below. They will appear overhead during the corresponding months.
