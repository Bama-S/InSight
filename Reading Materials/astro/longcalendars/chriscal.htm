CHRISTIAN CALENDAR

These calendars are purely solar in nature. The moon plays no role in this calendar. The year begins on January 1st and ends on December 31st. This is the calendar which is widely followed in the world at present. As we had noted, a normal year in this calendar consists of 365 days and a leap year which falls once in four years has 366 days. The moon plays no role in this calendar. The year begins on January 1st and ends on December 31st.

It is divided into 12 months. We have already learnt the names of the months and the number of days in each month. Christmas is a major festival of the Christians. It falls on December 25th, the birthday of Jesus Christ. Christians greet each other and exchange gifts during Christmas. Special trees called Christmas trees are decorated with lights.The Christmas mass in churches are of special significance. Easter is another important Christian festival. Easter Sunday is on a Sunday occuring immediately after the full moon following following March 21st.

Easter Saturday is the day before this and Good Friday is 2 days before Easter Sunday. The weekday is important in determining some Christian festivals
