Iranian Calendars

Iran at the time of tis great Emperor, Darius adopted the Egyptian calendar but took into account the correction of the defict of 1/4 days of a day in a year. They had all years of 365 days but used an intercalary month ( adhika maasa) of 30 days in a cycle of 120 years. the Iranian calendar in its essential form is still followed by the Parsees (in India), who are the descendants of the Iranians who took shelter in India.
