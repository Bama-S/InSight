MOTION OF THE SUN ALONG THE ECLIPTIC:

On some day the Star X and the sun S1 rise together at S1. They move above the horizon NWSE in the direction S1-S3. On the next day when the star X is rising the sun is still below the horizon at Y. It has to travel YS1' which is approximately 1o. This takes nearly 4 minutes.

On the third day when the star X is rising the sun is still further below the horizon at Z. It has to travel ZS2' which is 2 o. This takes nearly 8 minutes. The circle drawn is the ecliptic.
