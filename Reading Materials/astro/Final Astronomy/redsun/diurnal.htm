DIURNAL PATH OF THE SUN:

The diurnal motion of any celestial object is the path traced by the object in one day around the earth due to the rotation of the earth about it's own axis. For the sun, it's diurnal path on any day depends on the declination of the sun. The following cycle of variation keeps repeating year after year. The diurnal path keeps shifting to the North till the summer solstice when the sun has maximum northerly declination of 23 1/2 degree North. During this interval the days get longer and longer.

From the summer solstice to the autumnal equinox, the diurnal path reverses in direction and the Sun moves closer and closer to the celestial equator. Between summer solstice and autumnal equinox, the duration of the day keeps on decreasing.

From autumnal equinox to winter solstice the diurnal path keeps moving to the South. At the winter solstice the sun has the maximum southerly declination of 23 1/2 degree South. Between the autumnal equinox and the winter solstice the days continue to get shorter and shorter (less than 12 hours) till the duration of the day is the least and this occurs at the winter solstice.
